Before you upload your digital fit note

Check that your fitnote is valid

Your fit note should have the unique ID ‘Med 3’. If it’s missing or if it cannot be read by our system, your fit note could be rejected.

Where to find the Med 3 information?

Check the unique ID on the bottom left of your fit note.

If it contains a data matrix (a black and white square that looks like a QR code), it should be clear. If it cannot be read, your fit note will take longer to process.

Where to find the data matrix?

The data matrix is the small square of black and white dots at the bottom of your fit note.


Before you upload it, make sure:

  1. all the text can be read easily
  2. it is not protected by a password
  3. it is on 1 page with your contact details visible
  4. the whole fit note is visible, including all four corners

What to do if your fit note has a password

If you need to enter a password to see your fit note, our system will not be able to read it and it will be rejected.

If possible, you should save your fit note as a new file without a password and upload this new version.

You will need to enter your National Insurance number after you have uploaded your fit note. You can find this on your benefit letter, payslip or P60.
